Our mission
Philantropy is one of the most significant global flows of capital yet one of the least transparent

Oslo Philanthropic Exchange (OPX) brings the benefits of the global capital markets to philanthropy: efficient access to additional pools of capital, transparency & accountability.

  • Robust Due Diligence at Listing
  • Standarized Periodic Reporting
  • Blockchain Enabled
Oslo Philantropic Exchange one platform to filter, find, fund, and follow Philanthropy

Thousands of charities use third-party fund-raising firms, and their commissions can be so exorbitant—from 65 to 95 cents of every dollar raised—that they leave very little for the nonprofits to apply to their actual work.

Third-party fund-raising is perfectly legal, and many legitimate charities use it simply because it’s cheaper than having full-time fund-raisers on staff.

Overwhelming reporting diminishes impact

A substantial amount of documentation is submitted to donors with limited evidence that it is used.

NGOs consulted had an average of 36 reporting deadlines per country per year. For some, this was as high as 80. If ad-hoc requests are included, an NGO working in six countries estimated they would be submitting a report every 24 hours.

A significant proportion of reporting, Partner Capacity Assessment (PCA) and audit is regarded as duplicated effort that creates an inefficient use of resources. 100% of NGOs questioned agreed that their assessments could be shared between donors.

One international NGO calculated that it could save 11,000 hours on financial reporting for its nine largest donors if those donors agreed on cost definition and accepted the same finance template.

More of the money is going to the telemarketing firm than to the charity itself”
- Sandra Miniutti, Vice President, Charity Navigator
OPX solution

Our aim is to replicate the elements of the global capital markets that enable capital to flow seamlessly and efficiently on a global basis. We don’t need to re-invent the wheel. The system, for the most part, works.

With a few changes that address the specific needs of not-for-profit institutions, the same players can do the same jobs – but for philanthropy. Preparing companies for an OPX listing is not materially different from preparing companies for a listing on any of the world’s stock exchanges. Raising capital is the same – only for a different purpose and with a different story, a different return expectation. Research coverage, not marketing, but research regarding facts, comparability, impact, performance – this should also be the same.

Our aim is to enable long-term funding for long-term philanthropic missions. Unrestricted and not micromanaged. OPX have banks and broker dealers as Members, authorized to bring clients to a listing. OPX Advisors will be approved to conduct the business, legal and financial due diligence that is a requirement to any OPX listing. Only reputable institutions will gain membership into OPX either as Members or Advisors.

A professional approach to philanthropy – funding that matches the mission, access to capital that rewards performance, comparability across similar charities, easy access to relevant data, continuous access to newsflow and material events, periodic access to management reporting, availability of third-party research.
